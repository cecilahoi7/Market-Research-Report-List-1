<p><ul><li>Laser Diffraction</li><li>Dynamic light scattering (DLS)</li><li>Image Analysis</li><li>Coulter Principle</li><li>Nanoparticle Tracking Analysis</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The particle-size analyzers market encompasses various technologies for measuring particle size and distribution. Laser diffraction uses light scattering to determine size by analyzing patterns of scattered light. Dynamic light scattering (DLS) measures fluctuations in scattered light to assess smaller particles. Image analysis involves capturing images to assess particle size and shape. The Coulter principle measures electrical impedance changes as particles pass through an aperture. Nanoparticle tracking analysis visualizes and sizes nanoparticles based on Brownian motion. Other methods include ultrasound and sieve analysis.</p></p>
